Laptop Specifications

What Should You Look For?

There are a huge amount of manufacturers, as we previously mentioned, producing lots and lots of laptops with different specifications "also known as specs", these specs can vary from one laptop to another due to numerous reasons. we will now discuss these variations so you could pick the laptop that would fit for your needs.

Processors

Processors represent the brains of your PC. As a laptop user, you'll have two types of processors which are the CPU "also known as the central processing unit" and the GPU "also known as the graphics processing unit".

A CPU is the main processor which executes instructions in a computer program. The CPU performs basic arithmetic, logic, controlling, and input/output operations specified by the instructions in the program. CPU's largest manufacturers of all time are INTEL and AMD. Recently AMD has upped their game to become more of a tough opponent to INTEL. You can learn more about CPUs here.

A GPU is a specialized electronic circuit designed to rapidly manipulate and alter memory to accelerate the creation of images in a frame buffer intended for output to a display device. The top GPUs are mostly made by NVIDIA and AMD. GPUs shine best with NVIDIA's graphics cards but recently AMD has significantly improved their GPUs as well. You can learn more about GPUs here.

CPUs In Depth

When searching for CPUs, you may find them go by a lot of names from each manufacturer, which may be confusing at first but if you look closely you'll find a lot of similarities with just a few tweeks here and there. Let's start with INTEL CPUs for example :
  • i3-5005U
  • i5-6600K
  • i7-7700HQ
  • i9-9750H
As explained in the example above, we can find that INTEL Chip labels consists of :
  • A Brand Modifier shows the difference on one or more significant attribute or benefit dimensions. The higher the better but you must take the chip's generation into consideration as well which is clarified in the gen indicator below.
  • A Gen Indicator shows the latest and the most developed CPUs in processing and build "also known as the chip's architecture", which means higher generations will be superior.
  • SKU Numeric Digits just demonstrates how modified the chip is in the generation itself, it's considered a bit like minor upgrades in the chip's generation.
  • A Product Line Suffix may play a big role when understanding what type of processor you want to choose. It can be defined in the table on the right.
As for AMD CPUs, It's actually quite similar if we look closely, for example :
  • Ryzen 3-3100
  • Ryzen 5-5500U
  • Ryzen 7-4800H
  • Ryzen 9-5900HX
"Note : The (X) Suffix Refers to Xtreme."

GPUs In Depth

GPUs can make a lot of hussle when a new generation is released. However, don't rush into buying them as the changes may not be as big as you expect, it solely depends on the programs you use on a daily basis and their needs.

If you work with lots of photos or videos, a fast GPU is good for editing software. Plenty of well-known editing software applications take advantage of the added raw power of a GPU, including Adobe Photoshop, Lightroom and Premiere Pro (for serious photo editing). On a computer with a powerful GPU, it takes significantly less time to apply complex filters to photos and special effects to videos. That means less time waiting, more time creating.

But the upside is not confined to content creation apps. Web browsers such as Firefox, Chrome, and Edge can all tap into a GPU to improve page rendering times. Streaming video from YouTube and Netflix has to be compressed and decompressed before you actually see it. With a GPU, youâ€™ll probably run into fewer stutters or unwanted glitches as that process plays out.

With understanding what a GPU does, Let's see how can we pick a GPU that fits our needs, we'll be starting off with NVIDIA GPU labels and it consists of :
  • A Prefix which may vary between :
    RTX : Gives premium and the highest performance, Supports ray-tracing.
    GTX : Which provides a good performance with high standards.
    MX : which has a fine performance and works good with ultra-portable platforms.
  • A Generation points to the number of turing architecture of the GPU which gives a more satisfying gaming experience as it is capable of rendering real-time ray-tracing. The higher the turing architecture, the better.
  • Pref. Tier changes may be considered minor when observing the other labels, but don't let that fool you as a GPU with a better pref. tier may sometimes have a good impact on your experience without needing to upgrade to a whole different generation.
  • A Suffix is really simple, if it has "Ti" at the end of it then it will give a better performance overall.
When considering AMD GPUs, No need to panic. It's still honestly quite similar with just a few changes , for example :
  • A Prefix which may vary between :
    RX : Gives the highest performance overall
    R5 : Which provides a fine performance.
    R3 : which has a lower performance.
  • A Generation points to the series number where it's obvious that the higher series the better, especially the new 5000 series which hands down impressed everyone lately.
  • Pref. Tier changes shows you in what series range is the GPU chip.
    For example : 5500 means that it is a mid tier 5000 series chip.
  • A Suffix is still really simple, if it contains "XT" at the end of it then it is the chip's top model.

RAM & Storage

The Random Access Memory "Also known as RAM" is a computer's short-term memory. None of your programs, files, or Netflix streams would work without RAM, which is your computer's working space. It works in sync with the computer's processor as data is stored temporarily in it where the processor needs it.
This isn't to be confused with long-term data that's stored on your hard drive Storage, which stays there even when your computer is turned off.

Smartphone & Smartwatch Specifications

Are These Specs Different To The Laptop Ones?

The answer is No. Not at all. Maybe the technology's is a bit changed when taking a look at their sizes but they all function the same way with the same sole purpose of delivering the best performance possible.

Does It Really Matter In This Category?

Yes, it actually does matter as the manufacturers worldwide are trying their best to provide their users with the best experience possible on these smaller yet useful devices by upgrading their micro-chips on a whole other scale frequently.

Companies may even produce their own linup of chips to achieve their goals. As Apple did with their BIONIC Chips, and SAMSUNG did with their Exynos Chips.

Other companies may even not participate in the smart devices industry but will still make efficient chips for the companies that do, like Qualcomm with their very famous SNAPDRAGON Chips.

How Should I Choose In This Category?

In this category of devices, it is way easier. As all you have to do is to just focus on the software more than the hardware. See which interface makes you feel the most comfortable once you're in and serves you best. These small devices are supposed to be time-savers, so use that to your advantage and stay away from the devices that doesn't feel right to you.

So Hardware Isn't Important?

No, Hardware is still important, although the software plays a big part in these devices but it cannot work on it's own, it needs good hardware to support it.
If your budget allows it when buying a Smartphone, you should always go for :
  • Higher Refresh Rate Screens, more on that here.
  • Better Build Quality.
  • Cameras with both good hardware and software supporting it.
  • 4 GBs of RAM is enough. But more is fine.
  • 64 GBs of storage is good enough. If you need more storage, go ahead.
As for the Smartwatches, these are some useful tips worth mentioning :
  • Choose The Better Battery life.
  • Choose The Better Build Quality.
  • Get the ones with wide availability of apps and features.
  • Go for smartwatches that supports notifications and calls too if your budget allows it.
  • Take care when picking the wrist size. Don't ignore it.
